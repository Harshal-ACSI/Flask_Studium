What are Atoms?

To learn what atoms do, we need to learn what they exactly are. Atoms are the building blocks of matter. More specifically, they are the smallest constituent unit of matter that possess the properties of the chemical element.

Okay. We now know the meaning of an atom, but what exactly is it? An atom is made of three parts (called subatomic particles):
- Protons
- Neutrons, &
- Electrons
Let's dive deeper into the particles in an atom.

The Protons

The proton is a positively charged particle in an atom. Hence, we say that it has a charge of 1e. The word proton came from the Greek word, "prōtos" which means "first" and was given to the hydrogen nucleus by Ernest Rutherford in 1920 (a hydrogen nucleus only has one proton and nothing else!). A proton is also said to have the mass of 1.672 621 925 95 x 10-27 kg.[1]
